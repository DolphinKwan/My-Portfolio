Introduction Speaking of Chicago, the Bean, the museums and the charming view along the Skyline will always come to people’s mind. The city has offered me so much. However, the one thing most unforgettable and impressive to me is the scene of full moon rise I saw from the lakeshore Michigan. I came across this moon rise by accident but then it became the most precious memories of mine in Chicago. To dig into some trick and tips of watching the full moon. Let’s visit some basic knowledge about the moon. P.S. For the best experience, please use desktop browser.
The Daylight and the Night The Sun is like a giant fireball in the space. It lights up all the objects around it. Sunlight is always coming from one direction, half of the Earth will be bright, and the other half will be dark. We call it the bright side and the dark side of the Earth, or the Daylight and the Night. The line divides the bright and the dark side is called the terminator.
Day and Night The Earth is rotating along its own axis. In the ancient time, we called it "one day" based on the period during which The Earth makes one complete rotation on its axis. It's only because the axis of the rotation is in a proper orientation that we can experience both day and night without traveling around the world.
Relativity of the frame of reference The Earth is rotating from the West to the East. However, what we see every day is the Sun is Rotating the Earth from the East to the West. And why? It's because the relativity of the frame of reference. It's the same phenomenon when we are sitting in a train. Instead of feeling the cart is moving forward, we tend to feel that the trees along the road are moving backward.
